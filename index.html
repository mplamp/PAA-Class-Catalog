In this class, there is a fun, game-filled exploration of acting fundamentals, stage directions, and character development. The children apply their skills in hands-on learning through the making of a mini-musical, complete with lines, singing and dancing!In addition to discovering and artistically using the items in the show trunk, the students will also create crafts of their own in support of the musical. These may include props, costume-related pieces such as masks, and environmental support, such as drawings for scenery.

On the final day of class, we invite the young peoples' family and friends to join us for a sharing of the musical in a supportive classroom setting, complete with story-necessary props and costume pieces. The sharing will be kept simple, with the focus on the students and their creativity.

In addition, the students will participate in the all-class Shareformance at the end of the Fall classes, in which they will perform an ensemble number from the musical!

Voice Classes

(Studio Discovery)

Music is a powerful and playful tool. Learn how to play instruments and sing, creating music with your friends! This class will be more fun than you can shake a shaker at!

(Studio 3)

In Voice class, students work on developing the vocabulary of music as it is used in the art and practice of musical theatre. From foundational principles, to using music for storytelling and character journey, the emphasis is on practical application of music on the stage as a part of a larger theatrical narrative. Practice may include short solos similar to a monologue in music form, duets, and ensemble pieces. Homework of music review and preparation may be asked.

Technical Theatre Classes

(Studio Behind the Scenes)

Learn the art of creating the look and feel of a show through costuming. All abilities welcome. Sewing instruction will be included. You will make a piece to be worn in our current show.

(Studio Behind the Scenes)

The tiny things we carry and use in our daily lives make a huge difference, and a show is no different. Learn what it takes to create and source properties for a show. You will build props that will be used in our current show.

(Studio Behind the Scenes)

This course will explore what are projections, how they are created and how they are applied in theatrical environments. This course will aid in the basic understanding of projection design, the principles of composition, the elements of design, and the process of developing a conceptualized design idea as a designer.

(Studio Behind the Scenes)

Stage Design is for everyone! This class will introduce students to the basics of Theatrical Design. We will cover scenic design, costume design, lighting design and script analyses. This is a great class for acting and dancing students who want to learn more about tech and be ready for other areas of theatre like directing.

(Studio Behind the Scenes)

Learn the art of makeup creation. From a basic application to creating fantasy and gore, this class will give you the fundamental skills to remake a face as the director desires. Learn about highlight and shadow and play with dimension and color. All of your projects will be compiled into your very own makeup morgue!

(Studio Behind the Scenes)

Discover the process of designing costumes for a show from beginning to end. Learn how the time period impacts design and how set and lighting affect color choices. Hands on projects demonstrate both sewing machine and hand-sewing skills. Build costumes and accessories from scratch and be part of creating the magic!

(Pre-Req: Costuming)

(Studio Behind the Scenes)

There are many software programs available for the theatrical industry but a standard is Qlab. The class explores how Qlab works, how to program inside of it, and what you can do with it. It’s a great class for anyone looking to learn more about sound and projection programming for the theatre.

(Studio Behind the Scenes)

Prosthetics is a fun class that explores a unique aspect of Makeup! In this class you will learn how to use, apply and make prosthetics. Pictures are taken to exhibit progress and compiled into a portfolio demonstrating the process from start to finish. Come join us for a very exciting class!

Note: We will be working with Stage Makeup, foam, and latex in this class. (Pre-Req: Theatrical Make-up)

(Studio Behind the Scenes)

All beautiful costumes starts with an idea. In the world of theatre costumers use renderings to communicate their ideas with directors. In this class we will explore how to create costume renderings using various types of media.
